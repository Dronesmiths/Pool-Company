When Regular Cleaning Isn't Enough

Why Acid Wash Your Pool?

Over time, even well-maintained pools develop stubborn stains that regular brushing and chemicals can't remove. Algae, mineral deposits, calcium buildup, and organic staining penetrate the plaster surface and dull your pool's appearance.

An acid wash is a deep cleaning process that removes these embedded stains by treating the drained pool surface with a diluted acid solution. It's the most effective way to restore a stained, discolored pool to like-new condition.

Signs You Need Service

When Should You Acid Wash Your Pool?

Not every stained pool needs an acid wash. Here are the situations where acid washing is the right solution for restoring your pool's appearance.

Stubborn Algae Staining

Black algae or severe green algae that has stained the plaster even after chemical treatment and brushing. Surface discoloration that won't brush away.

Mineral & Calcium Deposits

Brown, rust, or white calcium scaling on walls and floor. Mineral staining from well water, hard water, or metal content in pool water.

Overall Discoloration

Pool surface has become dull, gray, or yellowed over years of use. Plaster looks aged and dingy despite regular cleaning and maintenance.

Before Replastering

Preparing for a pool party or special event and want the pool to look its best. Trying acid wash before committing to expensive replastering.

Post-Neglect Restoration

Pool was neglected, abandoned, or green for extended period. New homeowner wants to restore existing pool instead of replastering immediately.

Acid Wash FAQs

What is a pool acid wash?

An acid wash is a deep cleaning process that removes stubborn stains, algae, and discoloration from pool plaster using a diluted acid solution.

How often should I acid wash?

Most pools need acid washing every 3-5 years. Frequency depends on water chemistry, maintenance history, and staining severity.

How long does it take?

The complete process takes 2-3 days: 1 day to drain and wash, 1-2 days to refill and balance chemistry before swimming.

Will it damage my pool?

When done properly, acid washing is safe. It removes a thin layer of plaster (about 1/32 inch), so it should only be done when necessary.

What does it cost?

Pricing typically ranges from $400-$800 depending on pool size and stain severity. We provide exact quotes after inspection.

Can all stains be removed?

Acid washing removes most surface stains. Deep structural stains or severely damaged plaster may require replastering instead.
