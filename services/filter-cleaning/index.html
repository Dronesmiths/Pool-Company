Regular professional filter cleaning extends equipment life, improves water quality, and prevents expensive pump and motor failures caused by restricted flow.

Maintenance Schedule

How Often Should Filters Be Cleaned?

Filter cleaning frequency depends on pool usage, debris load, and local conditions. In the Antelope Valley's dusty, windy climate, filters work harder and need more frequent attention.

Cartridge Filters

Clean every 4-6 weeks during pool season. Monthly cleaning is recommended for pools with heavy debris or high use. Replace cartridges every 2-3 years.

DE Filters

Backwash and recharge with fresh DE every 4-6 weeks. Complete filter teardown and grid cleaning recommended once per year.

Pressure Monitoring

When filter pressure rises 8-10 PSI above clean baseline, it's time for cleaning. High pressure means restricted flow and reduced filtration.

Warning Signs

Cloudy water, reduced flow from return jets, longer run times, or algae growth can all indicate a dirty filter that needs immediate attention.

Filter Cleaning FAQs

How do I know my filter needs cleaning?

Check your pressure gauge. When pressure is 8-10 PSI higher than baseline, or if you notice cloudy water or reduced flow, it's time for cleaning.

Can I clean my own filter?

Yes, but proper cleaning requires the right tools and technique. We offer professional cleaning to ensure thorough results and equipment inspection.

Is filter cleaning included in weekly service?

Backwashing and basic maintenance are included. Deep cartridge cleaning is typically done as needed every 4-6 weeks.

When should I replace my filter?

Cartridge filters last 2-3 years with proper cleaning. DE grids last 5-7 years. We'll let you know when replacement is needed.

What's better: cartridge or DE filter?

Both work well. DE filters provide finer filtration, while cartridge filters are easier to maintain. We service both types professionally.
