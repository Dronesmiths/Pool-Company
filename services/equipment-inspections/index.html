Warning Signs

Problems We Catch Early

Regular equipment monitoring helps detect these common issues before they escalate into major failures requiring expensive emergency repairs.

Pump Motor Failure

Unusual noise, overheating, or intermittent operation signals impending motor failure. Early detection allows planned replacement instead of emergency breakdown.

Leaking Seals & O-Rings

Small drips around pump seals or filter o-rings grow into major leaks. We catch early moisture before it causes water loss and equipment damage.

Filter Pressure Problems

Abnormal pressure readings indicate clogs, air leaks, or valve issues. Weekly monitoring identifies circulation problems before water quality suffers.

Heater Ignition Issues

Pilot light problems, delayed ignition, or inconsistent heating signal developing heater failures. We spot warning signs and recommend service before total failure.

Timer & Automation Failure

Malfunctioning timers cause inadequate circulation and chemical imbalance. Regular checks ensure equipment runs on proper schedules.

Equipment Inspection FAQs

Is equipment inspection included?

Yes. Visual equipment checks are included with every weekly service visit at no additional charge. It's part of our comprehensive maintenance.

Do you repair equipment?

We identify problems and recommend qualified repair technicians. Our focus is maintenance and early detection, not equipment repairs.

What if you find a problem?

We'll alert you immediately and explain the issue. We can recommend trusted repair companies or help you understand repair vs. replacement options.

How often should equipment be replaced?

Pool pumps typically last 8-12 years. Heaters last 5-10 years. Salt systems 4-7 years. We'll let you know when equipment approaches end of life.

Can inspections prevent all failures?

Not all failures are predictable, but regular monitoring catches most developing problems early, dramatically reducing emergency breakdowns.
