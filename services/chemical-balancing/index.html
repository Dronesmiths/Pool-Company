What We Test & Balance Every Week

Our technicians test and adjust multiple chemical parameters during every weekly visit to maintain optimal water quality.

üß™ pH Level (7.2 - 7.8)

What it is: Measures how acidic or basic your water is.

Why it matters: Low pH corrodes equipment and irritates skin/eyes. High pH causes cloudy water and scale buildup.

How we fix it: Add pH decreaser (acid) or pH increaser (soda ash) to restore proper balance.

üíß Free Chlorine (1.0 - 3.0 ppm)

What it is: Active sanitizer that kills bacteria and prevents algae.

Why it matters: Low chlorine allows algae growth and bacteria. High chlorine irritates swimmers.

How we fix it: Add chlorine tablets, liquid chlorine, or shock treatment to maintain proper levels.

‚öñÔ∏è Total Alkalinity (80 - 120 ppm)

What it is: Buffer that stabilizes pH and prevents rapid swings.

Why it matters: Low alkalinity causes pH to fluctuate wildly. High alkalinity makes pH hard to adjust.

How we fix it: Add alkalinity increaser (baking soda) or lower with acid treatments.

‚òÄÔ∏è Cyanuric Acid / Stabilizer (30 - 50 ppm)

What it is: Protects chlorine from being destroyed by sunlight.

Why it matters: Without stabilizer, chlorine burns off in hours. Too much reduces chlorine effectiveness.

How we fix it: Add stabilizer when low. High levels require partial water drainage.

üî¨ Calcium Hardness (200 - 400 ppm)

What it is: Dissolved calcium in water.

Why it matters: Low calcium corrodes plaster and equipment. High calcium causes scaling.

How we fix it: Add calcium chloride to raise levels. Dilute with fresh water to lower.

Are chemicals included in service?

Can I test my own water?

Test strips give basic readings, but professional liquid reagent testing is more accurate. We test every week to ensure precision.
